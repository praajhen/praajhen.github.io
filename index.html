    About me

    I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Jyväskylä, Finland, specializing in multimodal physiological signal processing and biosignal algorithm development. My work focuses on integrating ECG, respiration, and EEG signals to extract phase-dependent features related to attention, learning, and cognitive processes. I develop signal processing pipelines for noisy physiological recordings, including cardiac cycle detection, respiration phase estimation, and multimodal synchronization.

    I completed my PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience at the University of Jyväskylä and previously obtained a Master's degree in Digital Communications from the University of Kiel, Germany, where I developed a strong foundation in signal processing. My research combines physiological understanding with algorithm design, with applications in wearable sensing, real-world biosignal analysis, and multimodal data integration.

    I have extensive experience working with EEG, ECG, respiration, and other physiological signals using MATLAB and Python. My work includes developing preprocessing pipelines, implementing feature extraction methods, and applying machine learning approaches for physiological signal analysis. I am particularly interested in developing robust biosignal algorithms for real-world wearable applications.
