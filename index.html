About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Jyväskylä. My recent research focuses on examining the impact of body rhythms (i.e., heartbeat and respiration) on learning and attention. I received a doctoral degree (PhD) in Cognitive Neuroscience from the University of Jyväskylä, Finland. I was an Early Stage Researcher and recipient of Marie Skłodowska-Curie Innovative Training Networks fellowship from the European Commission. I got Master Degree (M. Sc.) in Digital Communication from University of Kiel, Germany. I have worked as a visiting research fellow at various universities in EU and have experience in EEG, EMG, ECG, and MEG data analysis. Tasks: running lab experiments, collecting physiological data, publication, and training research assistances. I am skilled in programming languages and tools such as MatLab, SPSS, BESA, and Python. I am proficient in English and have intermediate Finnish language skills (B1).
